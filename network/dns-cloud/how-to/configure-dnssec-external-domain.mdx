DNSSEC uses digital signatures based on public key cryptography to strengthen DNS authentication. You can enable or disable DNSSEC for each of your external domain names.

<Message type="requirement">

- You have an account and are logged into the [Scaleway console](https://console.scaleway.com) 
- You have a external domain name managed with Scaleway Domains & DNS

</Message>

## How to activate DNSSEC

1. Click **Domains & DNS** in the **Network** section of the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. 

 The Domain & DNS dashboard displays. 

2. Click the domain name you want to configure.

3. Click **Activate** next to **DNSSEC** in the domain information section.

    <Lightbox src="scaleway-domain-dnssec.webp" alt="" />

4. Fill in the required information: 
    * ID 
    * Algorithm
    * Digest type
    * Public key

5. Click **Activate DNSSEC** to activate the configuration.

## How to edit or deactivate DNSSEC

1. Click **Domains & DNS** in the **Network** section of the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. 

  The Domain & DNS dashboard displays. 
  
2. Click the domain name you want to configure.

3. Click **Edit** next to **DNSSEC** in the domain information section.

4. Edit the DNSSEC information for your domain name, then click **Edit DNSSEC** to validate. 

5. Click **Deactivate DNSSEC** to deactivate DNSSEC.
